## Zoning & Legal Feasibility

### Resort Commercial Zoning Analysis

**Permitted Uses (Verify with Whatcom County):**
- ‚úÖ Restaurant and bar (current use)
- ‚úÖ Event venue / banquet facility
- ‚úÖ Hotel/lodging (potentially)
- ‚úÖ Private club (likely permitted as similar to restaurant)
- ‚úÖ Marine facilities (if developed)

**Required Actions:**
1. **Verify Private Club Allowed Under Current Zoning**
   - Contact Whatcom County Planning Department
   - May require conditional use permit
   - Public hearing possible (neighbor notification)
   - Timeline: 60-120 days

2. **Liquor License Conversion**
   - Current: Public restaurant/bar license
   - Need: Private club liquor license
   - Washington State Liquor and Cannabis Board approval
   - May be easier than public license (fewer restrictions)
   - Must demonstrate legitimate club operation

3. **Business License & Structure**
   - Form club entity: Non-profit or member LLC structure
   - Register with Washington Secretary of State
   - Obtain business license
   - Set up membership governance (bylaws, board)

4. **Building & Safety Permits**
   - Occupancy may need adjustment (fewer people, private)
   - Fire safety and ADA compliance
   - Any renovations require permits
   - Health department if full restaurant service

5. **Membership Requirements (Legal Structure)**
   - Must have legitimate membership selection process
   - Cannot discriminate on protected characteristics
   - Bylaws and operating agreement required
   - Board of directors/governors
   - Defined guest policies

### Private Club Legal Advantages

**Regulatory Benefits:**
- More control over capacity and operations
- Fewer public accommodation requirements
- Can be more selective about events/activities
- May have more flexible liquor service rules
- Privacy for members

**Tax Considerations:**
- Non-profit structure: Members own club, no profit motive
- For-profit structure: Shareholders own, club pays taxes
- Recommendation: For-profit LLC with member equity
  - Clearer ownership
  - Capital gains potential
  - Can distribute profits
  - Easier exit strategy

## Risk Analysis

### High-Priority Risks

**1. Membership Sales Shortfall**
- **Risk:** Don't sell enough founding/regular memberships
- **Impact:** Insufficient revenue, cash flow problems
- **Mitigation:**
  - Conduct thorough pre-launch market validation
  - Price competitively to drive demand
  - Flexible membership tiers (lower barrier options)
  - Strong sales and marketing effort
  - Offer incentives (early bird pricing, referral bonuses)

**2. Border Crossing Issues**
- **Risk:** Policy changes restrict cross-border travel
- **Impact:** Vancouver members can't access club regularly
- **Mitigation:**
  - Diversify membership base (Seattle, Bellingham, locals)
  - Focus on destination club appeal (worth the crossing)
  - Develop extended stay options (weekend packages)
  - Advocate for NEXUS/trusted traveler programs
  - COVID-19 showed risk - but also reopening resilience

**3. Insufficient Differentiation**
- **Risk:** Club not compelling vs. existing Vancouver/Bellingham options
- **Impact:** Weak sales, low retention
- **Mitigation:**
  - Emphasize unique assets (beach, waterfront, Point Roberts location)
  - Create signature experiences (events, member perks)
  - Build strong community and culture
  - Curate membership (quality over quantity)
  - Exceptional service and hospitality

**4. Operational Execution**
- **Risk:** Poor service, food quality, or member experience
- **Impact:** Member attrition, reputation damage, referral loss
- **Mitigation:**
  - Hire experienced club General Manager
  - Retain proven chef and core staff
  - Invest in training and culture
  - Member feedback systems
  - Continuous improvement mindset

**5. Financial Mismanagement**
- **Risk:** Overspend on improvements, insufficient reserves
- **Impact:** Cash flow crisis, can't sustain operations
- **Mitigation:**
  - Conservative budgeting
  - Phased improvements (use initiation fees)
  - Maintain 6-12 months operating reserves
  - Regular financial monitoring
  - Member dues sufficient to cover baseline operations

### Medium Risks

- Seasonal revenue concentration (summer peak)
- Competition from new club concepts
- Economic recession reducing discretionary spending
- Property maintenance needs (1959 building, waterfront exposure)
- Key staff turnover
- Regulatory changes (liquor, zoning)

### Risk Mitigation Summary

**Overall Risk Profile:** MODERATE

Club model is **lower risk than traditional restaurant** because:
- ‚úÖ Predictable revenue (annual dues vs. daily sales)
- ‚úÖ Lower volume = lower operating costs
- ‚úÖ Upfront capital from initiation fees
- ‚úÖ Curated membership = quality customers
- ‚úÖ Flexibility to adjust offerings based on member needs
